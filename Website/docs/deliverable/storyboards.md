# Storyboards (narrative, not interaction flow)

P5 leads. Each storyboard is a **situation**: who the person is, where they are, what they want, what friction they face, how HeritEdge fits. **No taps, no screens, no UI.**

Keep each to 4–6 frames. Write them like a short scene, not a spec.

## Task 1 — Conversational Heritage Guide

**Elena, 34, visiting from Bologna for the weekend.**

1. Saturday, July, 32 °C. Elena arrives at Duomo station with her niece Marta (11). They have fifteen minutes before meeting Elena's sister for lunch.
2. Elena has already been inside the cathedral once, years ago. She'd like to tell Marta *something interesting*, but she doesn't remember the details and Wikipedia feels wrong to read aloud.
3. She opens HeritEdge on her phone. She doesn't read tutorials; she taps the big "Ask" button and holds the mic: "What's the oldest part of the Duomo?"
4. The answer is two sentences, in conversational Italian. Elena repeats it to Marta, paraphrasing. Marta asks how the marble got there.
5. Elena reads the suggestion chip "Where does the pink marble come from?" and taps it. The answer mentions Candoglia; Marta notices the pink tint for the first time.
6. Her sister arrives. Elena closes the phone, keeps the conversation going at lunch.

*(Repeat pattern for Tasks 2 and 3 — one short narrative each, with a character and a situation.)*
