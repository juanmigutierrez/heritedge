# Image Sources — Duomo · Galleria · Palazzo Reale

Verified public-domain and openly-licensed photographs, paintings, and architectural drawings **of the three buildings themselves and their structural details** — façades, spires, mosaics, halls, domes, construction views, the Madonnina, the Sala delle Cariatidi. Organised by building and by chapter era.

For each entry: **what it is**, **where it lives**, **license**, and **how I'd use it in the app**.

> **General rule.** Pre-1900 works are reliably public domain. Wikimedia Commons explicitly labels every file's licence on its page. For modern photos (after 1955), look for **CC0**, **CC-BY** or **PD-self** in the licence box. The institutional repositories below are all open-access for educational/non-commercial use.

A "before and after" pair on the **Sala delle Cariatidi** would be the single most emotionally powerful moment in the entire Quick Guide. The Brogi photo from c. 1900 and a modern photo of the same room, side by side, tell the WWII story in two images without any text needed.

How to grab the highest-quality version from each source:

- **Wikimedia Commons** — open the file page, click **"Original file"** in the line *"Other resolutions: ..."*. Right-click → Save As.
- **Lombardia Beni Culturali** — image page has a download button; or right-click the large preview.
- **Pinacoteca di Brera (Open Brera)** — each work has *"Scarica in alta risoluzione"*.
- **Google Arts & Culture** — pinch-zoom in browser; for download use the page's three-dot menu → *Download image*.
- **Ambrosiana** — they provide hi-res via their *Codex Atlanticus* digital project; click the folio number to download the TIFF.

Once you place the JPEGs in `assets/history/`, I can:

1. Resize them to 1200 px wide WebP via a `sharp` build script.
2. Add them as backgrounds to the cinematic-hero panels in each chapter (the dark twilight panel already supports an image layer behind the dot grid).
3. Build the **before/after** Sala delle Cariatidi scene as a new scene type — a horizontal slider you drag to wipe between the two photos.

Just drop the files in and tell me which ones you've added.
